## PART 1 — Market Research Summary

**What buyers are searching for.** Three buyer streams converge on this product, each currently
served by *separate*, narrower listings on Etsy: (1) perimenopause/menopause symptom trackers —
a mature, crowded Etsy category with dedicated marketplace pages (`menopause_tracker`,
`perimenopause_daily_tracker`, `printable_menopause_symptom_tracker`); (2) GLP-1/peptide injection
trackers for semaglutide, tirzepatide, and compounded peptides — a newer, fast-growing category
(`glp_1_pdf_tracker`, `tirzepatide_tracker`, `peptide_journal_tracker`); (3) general biohacking/
wellness planners, a smaller but higher-price-tolerance niche. Nearly every listing found in
research is **single-purpose**: a menopause tracker doesn't track peptides, a GLP-1 tracker doesn't
track HRT or supplements. That gap is this product's core opportunity.

**Problems buyers want solved.** Symptom/dose amnesia ("did I take this Tuesday or Wednesday?"),
having nothing organized to bring to a doctor's appointment, juggling 3-4 separate apps/notebooks
for peptides + HRT + supplements + biohacking, not trusting apps with sensitive health data
(accounts, cloud sync, subscriptions), and reconstitution math anxiety (how many units of BPC-157
is 250mcg from *this* vial size?) — a problem almost no competing tracker addresses at all.

**Emotional desire that drives the purchase.** Feeling "in control" and "taken seriously" during a
life stage (perimenopause) or protocol (GLP-1/peptides) that often feels confusing, isolating, or
dismissed by clinicians. Buyers want to walk into an appointment with real data, not vague
recollections — and they want privacy while doing it.

**Overused listing angles.** Cutesy/coquette pastel aesthetics with heavy clipart; "before vs.
after" transformation photography; generic "track everything!" claims with no specifics; a wall of
undifferentiated checkbox pages.

**Underused angles that could stand out.** (1) *Format duality* — most competitors are PDF-only or
spreadsheet-only; almost none pair a printable binder with a real private web app. (2) *Specific,
countable inventory* — "36-entry peptide library," "84 supplements across 25 categories" reads as
far more credible than "track your supplements." (3) *Reconstitution math* — genuinely rare in this
category and directly answers a real, searched pain point. (4) *Privacy-first, no-account language*
— buyers in this niche are unusually privacy-sensitive (hormone data, off-label peptide use) and
almost no competing listing addresses it directly.

**Keyword saturation.** "Menopause tracker printable" and "symptom tracker printable" are heavily
saturated, broad, and dominated by established shops. "GLP-1 tracker" / "tirzepatide tracker" is
newer and less saturated but growing fast. "Peptide tracker printable" (non-GLP-1, e.g. BPC-157,
NAD+) is meaningfully less saturated — a smaller-shop opportunity.

**Better opportunities for a smaller/newer shop.** Long-tail, compound-specific, and combination
phrases: "peptide and menopause tracker," "biohacker planner for women," "reconstitution calculator
printable," "GLP-1 and HRT tracker" — lower volume individually, but far less competition and much
higher buyer-intent specificity.

**First 3 seconds the listing must communicate.** (1) This is a comprehensive system, not a single
sheet. (2) It covers peptides/GLP-1 *and* menopause/HRT *and* supplements — not just one. (3) It's
private (no accounts) and it's a one-time purchase, not another subscription.

**What AI search engines need to understand.** A precise, quotable definition ("a printable PDF
binder plus a private, no-account web app for tracking peptide and GLP-1 protocols, menopause/HRT
symptoms, supplement stacks, and biohacking routines in one system"), who it's for, what's
included (specific counts), and that it does not give medical advice.

**Questions buyers may ask AI before finding this product.** "What's the best way to track GLP-1
side effects and menopause symptoms in one place?" "Is there a printable tracker for peptides that
also has an app?" "What should I use to log my HRT and supplements without another subscription
app?" "How do I calculate peptide reconstitution dosing?"
